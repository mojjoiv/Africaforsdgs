Art For SDGs

Arts for SDGs focuses on how different artists will create awareness on sustainable community development. It will be based on different forms of art that’s; songs, paintings, and any kind that will help them communicate to their audience about SDGs. Talk of peace, activism and how we can engage in community solution creation to the issues affecting them either socially or political. It’s well-known that artists always have a following and if they can use this platform to help then others have a voice. Far from advocacy the program will look at ways to help the young upcoming artists have mentors who will help them grow into independence and focus on eradication of poverty. This is focused in implementation of target number 1.1, eradication of extreme poverty for all people everywhere. The initiative will have campaigns around the country with the artists where they can take part in fighting for rights based on these SDGs. The participants will be equipped with information focused under Sustainable development Goals, Vision 2030, Agenda 2063, and use of art in Governance. We are going to focus on the challenges and solution that African Youth face in their quest to achieve their goals and dreams under the vision 2030 Agenda, Big four agenda and the SDGs. The program gives chance to those willing to come together despite their different background but use art to have candid discussions that focus on the uniqueness in approach towards African problems and contexts. These will include focus on the problems that come with advocacy and awareness creation. The program also focuses on the implementation strategies of the goals and what are the challenges. Our 3 principles are to connect with one another, learn from one another and share with one another. The Hub is not a place to sign up, share your work and disappear. It’s an on-going commitment to working collaboratively and collectively to lift one another up. The Art for SDGs program is artist led and primarily for artists, although we welcome people from other backgrounds. We are inclusive. Our network is open to artists from all backgrounds, communities, experiences and qualifications. No one is better than the other.
